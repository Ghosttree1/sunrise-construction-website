## Voice notes

Write like someone who has nothing to prove. The reader is committing the most money, taste, and patience they'll ever spend in one place — they don't need persuading, they need to feel known. We position; we never pitch.

Cut any sentence that could live unchanged on another builder's site. Forbidden unless genuinely re-earned: *dream home, quality, experienced, transparent, reliable, turnkey, vision, passion, attention to detail.* Replace adjectives with facts — a named ridge, a real constraint, an honest number. Let silence and white space do work. The confidence is in what we leave out.

## Homepage — full flow

### Below the hero
A custom home leaves you exposed: money, taste, time, and family all on the table at once, for years. Sunrise exists to carry that weight with you and to make what's left at the end worth what it cost to get there. We build a small number of bespoke homes across Central Oregon, for people who care how a place is made.

### What we are
A legacy builder. Everything we put up is meant to be lived in for generations and handed down with its stories intact — and that single standard sets the rest: how we read a site, who we let near it, how we behave long after the keys change hands.

### The private client experience
No portal, no punch list standing in for a relationship. You work with the people answerable for your home, over years rather than phases. We keep the calendar short on purpose. Names, places, and details stay between us — assume discretion, never ask for it.

### Land and lifestyle
Central Oregon rewards the builders who understand it and exposes the ones who don't. We read aspect and wind, snow load and fire setback, water and well depth, the view corridor and the way late light comes off the Cascades — all of it, before a line is drawn. If the land isn't yet yours, bring us in early. The gap between two parcels is often the gap between the house you pictured and the one the site will permit.

### Architectural execution
We are who architects trust to protect the drawing. The reveal stays tight, the structure stays hidden, the proportion holds — intent survives the whole distance from rendering to room. When a design is ambitious, the execution is the work, and that is the part we answer for.

### Money and time, told straight
You'll have real numbers and a real schedule before you're committed, not after. Permitting, weather windows, trade availability, design review — these set the terms here, and we'd rather name them now than meet them mid-build. The clients who do best with us prefer the honest version, and so do we.

### Building within a community
The finest sites here often sit inside communities with their own standards and review boards. We know the requirements and the people who hold them, and we build to clear them without friction — so the home earns its place in its setting instead of fighting for one.

### After the home is built
We don't disappear at completion. We stay on for the life of the house — the seasonal systems, the settling, the change you'll want five years in. A home built to last generations needs someone who remembers how it was made. We keep that memory.

### For architects and realtors
A referral you can put your name beside. Architects send us the work that has to be executed exactly. Realtors send the client weighing raw land against a build, who needs a straight read on what a parcel can become and what it will truly cost. We guard your relationship as closely as our own.

### Closing
If a home in Central Oregon is on your mind, the first conversation costs nothing and clarifies a great deal.
