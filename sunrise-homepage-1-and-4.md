## Section 1 — The land speaks first
*Header:* **The land speaks first.**

Before a line is drawn, we listen to the site — the aspect, the wind, the way late light comes off the mountains and the cold settles in the low ground at night. Central Oregon rewards the builders who understand it and exposes the ones who don't. We learn a place before we touch it.

*Microcopy under image:* Aspect. Wind. Water. Light. The house answers to all four.

*→ Start with the site*

---

## Section 2 — What we make
*Header:* **Stone, timber, glass, and a long horizon.**

We build a small number of homes meant to be lived in for generations and handed down with their stories intact. That single standard sets everything upstream — how we read the ground, who we let near it, how we behave long after the keys change hands.

*Pull quote:* "A home that looks like it grew out of the ridge, not onto it."

---

## Section 3 — Execution
*Header:* **Ambition, held to the millimeter.**

We are who architects trust to protect the drawing. The reveal stays tight, the structure stays hidden, the proportion holds — intent survives the whole distance from rendering to room. When a design is ambitious, the execution is the work, and that is the part we answer for.

*→ See the work*

---

## Section 4 — The honest terms
*Header:* **The high desert keeps its own schedule.**

Weather windows, permitting, water, design review, the trades who can do this caliber of work — these set the terms here. You'll have real numbers and a real timeline before you're committed, not after. We'd rather name the constraints early than meet them mid-build.

---

## Section 5 — After the build
*Header:* **We don't leave the ridge.**

The relationship doesn't close at completion. We stay on for the life of the house — the seasonal systems, the settling, the change you'll want years in. A home built to last generations needs someone who remembers how it was made.

---

## Section 6 — For architects & realtors
*Header:* **The people who send us their hardest work.**

Architects send us the projects that have to be executed exactly. Realtors send the client weighing raw land against a build, who needs a straight read on what a parcel can become and what it will truly cost. We guard those relationships as closely as our own.

## Section 1 — The premise
*Header:* **A short list of homes. A long relationship with each.**

A custom home leaves you exposed — money, taste, time, and family on the table at once, for years. We take on few projects so that yours is never competing for attention. You work with the people answerable for your home, over years rather than phases.

---

## Section 2 — Discretion
*Header:* **Assume discretion. You won't have to ask for it.**

No portal standing in for a relationship. No project we'd name without your leave. Names, places, and the particulars of how you live stay between us. Much of our work is known only to the families it was built for.

---

## Section 3 — Land
*Header:* **We read the ground before we draw on it.**

Central Oregon is unforgiving of assumptions. Aspect, wind, water and well, fire setback, view corridor, the standards of the community a parcel sits within — we understand all of it before a line exists. If the land isn't yet yours, bring us in early; the gap between two parcels is often the gap between the house you pictured and the one the site will permit.

*→ Begin with the site*

---

## Section 4 — Execution
*Header:* **The drawing is a promise. We keep it whole.**

We are the partner architects rely on when a design cannot afford to lose anything in the field. The reveal holds, the structure hides, the proportion stays true. Ambition is only as good as its execution, and execution is what we answer for.

---

## Section 5 — Terms, plainly
*Header:* **The numbers and the years, told straight.**

You'll have real figures and a real schedule before you are committed. Permitting, weather, the trades capable of this work, community design review — we name these at the start. The clients who do best with us prefer the honest version. So do we.

---

## Section 6 — Community
*Header:* **A home that earns its place.**

The finest sites here sit within communities that keep their own standards. We know the requirements, and the people who hold them, and we build to clear them without friction — so the home elevates its setting rather than fighting for one.

---

## Section 7 — Afterward
*Header:* **We stay.**

Completion is not the end of the relationship. We remain the care partner for the home we built, for as long as it stands — because a house meant to be handed down needs someone who remembers how it was made.

---

## Section 8 — Referrals
*Header:* **For architects and realtors.**

Architects: when the design can't be compromised on site, we keep it intact. Realtors: send the client weighing land against a build — they'll get a straight read on the parcel, the number, and the years, and you'll keep a partner who protects the relationship.
