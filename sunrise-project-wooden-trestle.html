Modern and clean-lined — a Brasada Ranch home built to open completely to the outdoors.

Set within the gates of Brasada Ranch in Powell Butte, Wooden Trestle is a contemporary take on high-desert living: crisp lines, walls of glass, and a bright, open interior with almost none of the heavy stone the setting might suggest. At its heart is a turf courtyard, wrapped by the home and opened to it through oversized glass garage doors that fold the living spaces straight into the outdoors.

A soaring great room and an open kitchen anchor the main house, with a children's bunk room for the crowd and a separate ADU for guests or multigenerational living. It's built for the way families actually gather at Brasada — flexible, light-filled, and made to blur the line between inside and out.
